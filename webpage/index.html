Whisper of Heart - about passion, young love & imposter syndrome

About:

The 1995 film Whisper of the Heart adapted from the manga of the same name is about a young girl, Shizuku Tsukishima, preparing for the middle
school exams that determine the college that one goes to in high school. We see her journey through finding a passion and striving for perfection
it, navigating the young love that stumbles upon the way and some supportive people that help our girl through it.

The movie form the start has the calm vibes of a Studio Ghibli film unlike others it does not have have heavy magic elements but is more grounded
in the magic of human experience. The movie highlights the tough journey that every artist has in this society. Despite the very supportive
elders and friends Shizuku does doubt her passion and dreams as a young writer and fears failure. It also have that light middle school fun,
and themes of young love.

Passion and Dreams:

A little girl in her room with her nose in books, this is how the journey of most budding authors begins . We can see it in Shizuku’s
thirst for a magical adventure that she is waiting to be whisked away into one and thus follows the cat that she thinks is magical.
My favorite scene would have to he the scene where Shizuku for the first time shows the first draft of her first novel to Seiji’s
grandfather, the burst of tears at the approval and the revelations of doubt and failure contribute to a very emotional scene that
most people can relate to. On the other hand, the story of Seji, who knows just what he wants to do and taking all the right steps needed
to fulfil his passion and dreams but still having the doubts of not being good enough. Both the stories have the rawness of youth in it.

Love Life and Parents:

Towards the end of the middle school every one would have their experience with crushes, nosy friends and lots of one-sided love.
As the story goes we see the potential love growing between Seji and Shizuku , despite their initial differences both seem to be
more compatible than we think. They complement each other really well, always encouraging and motivating the other. Through Seiji’s
determination we witness Shizuku feeling empowered too. In a scene where Seiji laboriously tries to cycle on an uphill road with
Shizuku on the back, she quickly gets down, saying “I am no man’s burden! I want to be helpful.” pushing it along, ensuring they
both enjoy the moment equally.
We also witness that Shizuku has very supportive parents, even though they do not understand he dream do not pressure
her into anything but also educate her that she will be the sole bearer of the consequences of the choices in life.
